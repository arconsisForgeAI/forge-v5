FAQ.

Who is AMP for?

AMP is built for enterprises where modernization cannot fail quietly. Banking, insurance, telecommunications, the public sector, regulated industries, and energy.

Typical characteristics include:

  • auditability and compliance requirements,
  • long-term ownership and maintainability of the system after modernization,
  • and concerns about cloud vendor lock-in from procurement, governance, or regulatory perspectives.

If a modernization initiative can accept a managed-cloud, vendor-bound, or per-LOC pricing model, hyperscaler offerings such as AWS Q Transform, GitHub Copilot App Modernization, or IBM watsonx Code Assistant for Z may already cover much of the required scope.

AMP is designed for the migrations those tools cannot cleanly support, or for organizations where their operating or licensing models are not a fit.

What models and LLMs do you use, and who provides the accounts?

We typically work with leading frontier models such as OpenAI and Anthropic Claude. Our approach is intentionally LLM-agnostic to avoid vendor lock-in and to choose the best model for each modernization scenario.

We also evaluate open-source and on-premise models. In our experience, frontier models currently deliver the best results for complex modernization and transformation tasks, especially in the early phases of a modernization initiative.

Account and API ownership depend on the engagement model:

  • For smaller engagements or discovery sprints, we often use our own managed accounts and API keys, always covered by appropriate DPAs.
  • For enterprise and regulated environments, customers usually provide their own accounts, subscriptions, and API keys to meet security and compliance requirements.

In both cases, careful management of token consumption, context handling, and data governance is essential and jointly managed as part of the project setup.

Do you need access to internal systems?

Direct access to internal systems enables deeper automation and integration capabilities, but is often not required during the early discovery and assessment phases.

The required level of access is typically clarified during the Discovery and Technical Fit Workshop.

In many cases, we can start with:

  • exported repositories,
  • copied artefacts,
  • documentation snapshots,
  • or pipeline exports.
What are the critical success factors for modernization projects?

For the type of modernization projects we address with AMP, two factors are especially critical.

First, a clear target picture: what exactly should be modernized, to what extent, and with which architectural and business goals in mind?

Second, systematic enablement of the customer team. A common pitfall is that the project team builds a highly modern system, but the customer team remains dependent on external experts because architecture, AI, and delivery capabilities were not transferred early enough. That is why modernization, enablement, and handover should run in parallel from the very beginning.

From a customer development team perspective, this typically means a gradual transition of ownership:

  • Agents and AI workflows: from simply using agents, to adapting workflows, to creating and operating new agents independently.
  • CI/CD and delivery: from understanding the delivery pipeline, to adapting and extending it, to full operational ownership.
  • Codebase and architecture: from participating and pairing in development, to implementing own features, to owning the codebase, software design, and software architecture.
